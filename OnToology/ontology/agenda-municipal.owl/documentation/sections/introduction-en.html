Introduction back to ToC

This vocabulary represents the information of the municipal agenda that includes meetings of collegiate bodies such as the Plenary and the Governing Board and meetings in general, events and meetings with the media with participation of the mayor, councilors, directors or temporary personnel during the exercise of their position in the city council. This vocabulary has been developed in the context of the "Open, collaborative and interoperable government platform" project, in which the city councils of Madrid, Zaragoza, Santiago de Compostela and A Coru√±a participate. The vocabulary reuses terms from the vocabularies [schema.org](http://schema.org/) and [foaf](http://xmlns.com/foaf/0.1/) which include concepts and relationships that are used in similar settings. Additionally, it refers to terms of other models for the representation of domain concepts such as organizations, administrative divisions and municipal facilities. Specifically, for the representation of the events, the places where they are carried out and their doucments, the schema.org ontology is reused. The foaf vocabulary is reused to represent the agents that can be holders, conveners or assistants to an agenda event. The vocabulary of [organizations](https://www.w3.org/TR/vocab-org/) is reused to represent the positions held by the people who work in the city council and the administrative units to which they belong. Also, the [postal address](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/urbanismo-infraestructuras/direccion-postal/index-en.html) and the [streetmap](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/urbanismo-infraestructuras/callejero/index-en.html) vocabularies are reused to represent the normalized address of an event. Finally, the vocabulary of [territory](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/sector-publico/territorio#) contains the governmental administrative divisions that are used to represent the scope of competence of an administrative unit, for example, a municipality in general or a certain district.
